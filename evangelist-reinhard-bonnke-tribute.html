Evangelist Reinhard Bonnke

The man who won over 80 million souls to Christ

Here's a chronology of Evangelist Reinhard Bonnke's life:

  • Born on 19 April 1940 in Königsberg, East Prussia, Germany.
  • Became a Born-Again Christian at age 9 after his mother preached to him about a sin he had committed.
  • At age 10, young Reinhard discerned the call of God upon his life to serve as a missionary in Africa.
  • In 1951, Reinhard lead his first soul to Christ on a street corner in Glückstadt.
  • Reinhard attended the Bible College of Wales founded by Rees Howells.
  • Pastored in Germany for 7 years after his graduation.
  • In 1964, Reinhard married his wife Anni and began his family.
  • In 1967, the Bonnkes by the Velberter Missions Board were given a one-year South African apprenticeship with the Apostolic Faith Mission (AFM) which would cover the then Transvaal and also Swaziland.
  • In 1968, Reinhard became the AFM overseer of three small churches serving the indigenous Basuto people in the Kingdom of Lesotho after completing his apprenticeship.
  • In 1970, Reinhard devised an employment plan for the unemployed young men called the Soweto Bicycle Campaign to also spread the Gospel.
  • In 1973, Reinhard had a recurring dream featuring a picture of the map of Africa being splashed and covered with the blood of Jesus and also heard the words "Africa Shall Be Saved".
  • In 1974, Christ for all Nations was formed with headquarters in Johannesburg.
  • In 1976, Reinhard's meetings proved that the rental tents available in South Africa were far too small. The Lord spoke to Reinhard to trust Him for a 10,000 seat tent which in trusting God's word was later constructed by a tent maker in Milan.
  • With the infux of people, the Holy spirit spoke to Reinhard to trust the Lord for 34,000 seat tent.
  • In 1984, the colossal tent was built entirely by CfaN technicians, which according to the Guinness Book of World Records was the largest mobile structure on earth at the time.
  • In 1986, the colossal tent was retired from CfaN ministry due to a larger influx of people and was given to other missions organisations in Africa who use it for evangelisation of the continent.
  • The subsequent Gospel Campaigns were conducted in open-air using stadiums or vast open areas that were cleared and set up specifically for the campaigns.
  • In 1990, CfaN's campaign in Kaduna, Nigeria attracted a congregation of 500,000 people who gathered to hear the Gospel. CfaN meetings in Kaduna concluded with a total attendance of 1,670,000 people which was more than the number they preached to during the entire year of 1987.
  • In 1999, Reinhard Bonnke and the Christ for all Nations team were invited back into the country by president-elect Olusegun Obasanjo after nine years of forced absence from Nigeria.
  • In October 1999, Reinhard arrived in Benin City, the capital of Edo State and held a six-day event that was attended by almost 2 million people in a city with a population of just 800,000 people! 396,000 decision cards for salvation were received!
  • In 2000, CfaN held the Fire Conference in Lagos, Nigeria. Over 1.6 Million people attended the final night. 3,461,171 salvations were recorded and nearly 4 million books given out.
  • In 2003, More than 7,571,987 souls embraced the saving power of Jesus Christ through CfaN campaigns in Nigeria.
  • In 2007, young Evangelist Daniel Kolenda joined CfaN. After awhile, he started preaching before Reinhard would in some of the campaigns and later preached on some campaigns entirely alone.
  • By end of 2011, CfaN had recorded 60,862,305 salvations in Nigeria!!
  • Reinhard passed on th baton to Daniel who now serves as the president and CEO of Christ for all Nations. And under his leadership, CfaN is pressing on with a commitment to leverage every technology and opportunity God makes available for the great cause of winning souls.
  • Evangelist Reinhard Bonnke won over 80,029,661 Documented souls to Christ.
  • Went to be with the Lord on 7 December 2019 at age 79
